About Me

Outside of my day job as a global sales manager for a technology company, I have TONS of fun helping youth in STEM endeavors. Along for this journey are a wonderfully supportive partner, 3 great children, and other fantastic like-minded families. This incredible group has enabled endeavors including 42 robotics teams, 50+ outreach events, Women In Engineering Day at Liberty Science Center 2018 & 2019, and NJ World Robot Olympiad (WRO) competitions 2017, 2018, 2019. Although COVID19 robbed us of the 2020 & 2021 events, we are planning to come back strong in 2022 with Women In Engineering Day and NJ WRO. To restart that momentum for in-person events, I recently created a non-profit entity, American Robotics Open Championship, this year to help advance opportunities for young STEM'ers across the country (check back for updates on this!)

As a volunteer coach for neighborhood robotics teams since 2010, I've had the honor of working with 63 talented kids who competed in 141 competition events spanning 42 robotics campaign seasons and 14 competition types. We have been blessed with highly motivated and passionate kids who strive to do their very best individually and as team members, resulting in 80 team awards, with 10 at the national/international level. 

Learning takes place in three locations: our makerspace, robotics competition venues, and STEM outreaches to underrepresented and disadvantaged communities. 

A majority of our teams work in our makerspace, programming robots and discussing teamwork, good stewardship, and core values of who/what we are. At robotics competition venues, students learn the merits of teamwork as they pair up to compete against other pairs of teams. Before COVID-19 hit, our teams typically complete about 10 outreaches per year to help promote robotics and STEM to underrepresented and disadvantaged communities.

With a BSEE, MS-IT, and MBA, I worked at companies ranging from small startups to technical behemoths like AT&T Bell Labs. Spending the last 16 years in technical sales, I have had the privilege of meeting talented engineers, living out their dreams designing and bringing to market many incredible products. I grew up and started working in our family restaurant at age 7. I saw how difficult life was for people without college degrees or technical skills. Through those experiences, I learned the value of hard work and giving back to the community through leadership and mentorship. Through 11 years, it has been and continues to be an amazingly rewarding experience watching kids develop skills in STEM, communication, and teamwork.
