Exit 5 Robotics

Founded in 2010, the all-girls Exit 5 Robotics rapidly became known as a high achieving robotics team rather than an all-girls robotics team. Driven by a core group with laser focused determination, they had a chip on their shoulder and something to prove. 

Exit 5 Robotics being the first all-girls NJ Champions Award Winners at the 2011 NJ State FLL Finals shocked the NJ community of parents, coaches, and educators. That was a 15 year and 1800 team barrier that was shattered. When they repeated with Champions Award again 2012 and again in 2013 it firmly embedded the notion of a top-tier all-girls robotics team. It utterly shattered the stereotype that robotics was a "boys only" competition. 

The all-girls robotics team concept was still so novel back in 2014, that we were featured in a mic.com article "Meet the Young Women Helping to Build the Future of Robotics." and also featured in a June 2013 IEEE Women In Engineering magazine article.

In fact from 2012 through 2016, Googling "all-girls robotics" returned Exit 5 Robotics as the 2nd ranked search result across the US. 1st ranked result was Pittsburgh’s awesome Girls of Steel FRC team. The number of all girls robotics teams in NJ skyrocketed from 4% of teams in 2010-2013 time frame to almost 35% by 2020 and we believe that teams like ours, Girls of Steel, and others helped to power that growth of all-girls teams.
