NJ WRO (World Robot Olympiad) Qualifiers 2017, 2018, 2019, 2022

Bill Lam

Back in 2015, we heard about an incredibly novel competition called World Robot Olympiad where kids build their robots in the morning and then compete in the afternoon of a single day competition. Entranced by the idea, we entered the competition with two teams, Exit 5 Robotics and Exit 5A Robotics. Because we didn't have a local WRO qualifier in NJ we had to go through an video submission for one team and travel to NY for the other team to compete against another team. 

In 2016, we had a similar qualifying experience and realized that NJ needed a qualifier. With the NJ robotics landscape of FLL, FTC, VIQ, and VEX, I started laying the groundwork for an entirely new to NJ competition that was WRO. 

From securing the venue (Liberty Science Center), to enlisting a bevy of about 15 enthusiastic volunteers, creating trophies, and so much more... we were able to find 11 interested teams to compete in the inaugural NJ WRO Qualifier in 2017. Following the FLL qualifier model, NJ WRO was powered entirely by unpaid volunteers and has been the case every year since.

Building upon that initial success, we grew the 2018 NJ WRO Qualifier by about 40% out to 18 teams and 20+ volunteers from local FLL, FTC, and VEX teams. The 2019 NJ WRO Qualifier grew again by about 40% to 33 teams and almost 40 volunteers.

There was just so much to learn in all this from the dedicated volunteers from Livingston Robotics Club, Emerald Knights, LSC, and so many other groups. That included providing opportunities for young adults to try new responsibilities they had never experienced before. I don't know if there is any greater joy than watching a kid DJ for the first time, seeing a 15 year old find their voice to become a commanding emcee by days end, or witnessing a budding content creator's first event highlight video. 

The 2020 & 2021 NJ WRO events were COVID cancellations and we fully expect to be back and running NJ WRO in 2022!
